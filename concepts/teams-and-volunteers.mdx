## Teams

A **Team** is a group of participants competing in an Edition. The team's `name` is its primary key — it must be unique across the entire system.

### Team Fields

| Field | Type | Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| `name` | `String` (PK) | Required; 3–50 characters |
| `city` | `String` | Required; max 100 characters |
| `foundationYear` | `Integer` | Required; must be ≥ 1998 |
| `educationalCenter` | `String` | Optional; max 100 characters |
| `category` | `String` | Required, non-blank |
| `inscriptionDate` | `LocalDate` | Auto-set on creation; must be past or present |

<Note>
Because `name` is the primary key, team names are immutable after creation.
</Note>

---

## Team Members

A **TeamMember** represents an individual participant on a team.

| Field | Type | Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | `Long` | Auto-generated |
| `name` | `String` | Required; 2–100 characters |
| `birthDate` | `LocalDate` | Required; must be in the past |
| `gender` | `String` | Optional; max 20 characters |
| `tShirtSize` | `String` | Optional; max 10 characters |
| `role` | `String` | Required; max 50 characters |
| `team` | `Team` | Required foreign key |

<Warning>
A team cannot have more than **10 members**. Attempting to add an eleventh member will throw an error.
</Warning>

---

## Coaches

A **Coach** is an adult supervisor linked to one or more teams.

| Field | Type | Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | `Integer` | Auto-generated |
| `name` | `String` | Required, non-blank |
| `emailAddress` | `String` | Required, unique, valid email |
| `phoneNumber` | `String` | Optional |

### Assignment Rules

Coach assignment is subject to two hard limits enforced in `Team.addCoach()`:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Max coaches per team" icon="users">
    A team can have at most **2 coaches**. Adding a third raises `MAX_COACHES_PER_TEAM_REACHED`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Max teams per coach" icon="user">
    A coach can train at most **2 teams**. Assigning a coach already training 2 teams raises `MAX_TEAMS_PER_COACH_REACHED`.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Floaters

A **Floater** is a volunteer (see below) who provides support directly to teams during the event. Teams have a separate many-to-many relationship with floaters.

<Warning>
A team can have at most **2 floaters** assigned. Attempting to assign a third will raise an error.
</Warning>

---

## Volunteers

The `Volunteer` entity is the abstract base for all event staff. All volunteer types share these fields:

| Field | Type | Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | `Long` | Auto-generated |
| `name` | `String` | Required, non-blank |
| `emailAddress` | `String` | Required, unique, valid email |
| `phoneNumber` | `String` | Required, non-blank |
| `edition` | `Edition` | Foreign key — the edition this volunteer is assigned to |

Volunteers use **joined-table inheritance** (`InheritanceType.JOINED`). Each subtype has its own table with additional fields.

### Volunteer Hierarchy

```
Volunteer (abstract base)
├── Judge
├── Referee
└── Floater
```

---

## Judge

A **Judge** evaluates scientific projects during the competition.

| Additional Field | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `expert` | `boolean` | Whether the judge is a domain expert |
| `memberOfRoom` | `ProjectRoom` | The project room panel this judge belongs to |

A `ProjectRoom` has a managing judge (`managedByJudge`) and a list of panelist judges.

---

## Referee

A **Referee** supervises matches on a competition table.

| Additional Field | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `expert` | `boolean` | Whether the referee is a senior/expert referee |
| `supervisesTable` | `CompetitionTable` | The table this referee is assigned to (max 3 referees per table) |

<Note>
A `CompetitionTable` can have a maximum of **3 referees** assigned to it.
</Note>

---

## Floater (Volunteer Subtype)

A **Floater** is a student volunteer who assists teams during the event.

| Additional Field | Type | Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| `studentCode` | `String` | Required, unique |
| `assistedTeams` | `Set<Team>` | Teams this floater is assigned to |

<Tip>
Floaters carry a `studentCode` that uniquely identifies them in the system — this is separate from their `emailAddress`.
</Tip>

---

## Role Conflict Validation

Since `Judge`, `Referee`, and `Floater` are **separate entity types**, a single person cannot hold two roles in the same edition without creating two separate volunteer records. The email address column has a `unique` constraint on the `volunteers` table, so the same email address cannot be registered as both a Judge and a Referee (or any other combination) within the system.

<Warning>
A volunteer's `emailAddress` is globally unique across all volunteer roles. You cannot register the same person as both a Judge and a Referee, even in different editions.
</Warning>
